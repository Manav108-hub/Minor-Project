About

Ecosystem knowledge and preservation have benefited greatly from increased monitoring of biodiversity in recent years. For these initiatives to be successful, accurate and timely species identification is essential because it enables scientists and conservationists to monitor alterations in species populations, spot threats to biodiversity, and take preventative action to save endangered species. Conventional techniques for species identification often require the manual inspection of experts, which can be costly, time-consuming, and prone to human error. This process can be automated to increase its efficiency and scalability, thanks to the rapid advancements in machine learning and artificial intelligence (AI). The goal of this research is to create an automated species recognition system using AI. The system will learn to recognize species by using Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), a type of deep learning model.
